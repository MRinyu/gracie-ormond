Instructors

Professor John-Michael Kaleikini

Professor John-Michael Kaleikini is one of the select few black belt professors personally awarded by Grandmaster Relson Gracie. With nearly 30 years of dedicated training in Gracie Jiu-Jitsu, Professor Kaleikini has trained directly under the legendary Relson and Rickson Gracie, gaining rare insight into the art’s original techniques and philosophies.

In addition to his black belt in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, Professor Kaleikini holds black belts and instructor certifications across several martial arts disciplines:

  • American Kickboxing Black Belt under Dennis Alexio
  • Taekwondo Black Belt under Laymon "Flame" Williamson
  • Muay Thai Kru Certification under Master Kam
  • Dutch Kickboxing Kru Certification under Willem Van Dyke
  • Certified Ginástica Natural Instructor

At Gracie Florida, Professor Kaleikini is committed to teaching authentic Gracie Jiu-Jitsu and fostering a welcoming, growth-driven environment. His instruction emphasizes technical excellence, functional self-defense, personal development, and the timeless values of respect, discipline, and perseverance.

Professor Chet Quint

Professor Chet Quint is a seasoned martial artist with over three decades of experience. He holds black belts in multiple disciplines, including Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, Taekwondo, and Goju Ryu Karate, and is a certified instructor in Muay Thai. His journey in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu began in 1996 under the tutelage of Professor Pedro Sauer in Salt Lake City, Utah. In 2004, he deepened his expertise by training full-time in Brazil with Gracie family members Rilion and Rolker Gracie.

Professor Quint has been instrumental in spreading Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu globally, notably establishing academies in Beijing and Shanghai, and serving as the head BJJ instructor at Gracie China. His diverse background also includes experience as a police officer and soldier, enriching his practical approach to self-defense training.

At our academy, Professor Quint is dedicated to teaching authentic Gracie Jiu-Jitsu, emphasizing both the art's technical aspects and its core values of discipline, respect, and continuous learning.

FAQ

What is Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ)?

Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu is a grappling and self-defense martial art. It’s both a competitive sport and a practical self-defense system. At our academy, you’ll learn striking, takedowns, grappling, submissions, and sport BJJ structure.

Do I need prior experience to start training?

Nope! Everyone starts somewhere — and we love helping beginners find their footing.

What should I wear to my first class?

Flexible workout clothes are fine for your first class! No baggy clothes or shoes on the mats.

Do you offer trial classes?

Yes! We offer a free trial class and you’re welcome to visit our Friday Open Mat at 7:30 PM.

Is Jiu-Jitsu safe for kids?

Yes! Kids build confidence, fitness, friendships, and valuable self-defense skills through Jiu-Jitsu.
